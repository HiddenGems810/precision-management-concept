# Marketing & Brand Compliance Guardrails

Due to the nature of property management and multifamily real estate, all brand and marketing materials must strictly adhere to Fair Housing laws and professional ethical standards.

## 1. Fair Housing Neutral Language
Marketing language must never imply preference, limitation, or exclusion based on race, color, religion, sex, handicap, familial status, or national origin.

**Avoid (High Risk):**
- "Perfect for young professionals"
- "Ideal for families"
- "No children"
- "Safe neighborhood" (can be interpreted as code for exclusionary practices)
- "Exclusive community"
- "Christian community"
- "Best for singles"
- "Family-friendly only"

**Use (Neutral & Professional):**
- "Professionally managed"
- "Convenient access to [transit/highways]"
- "Clear leasing process"
- "Responsive communication"
- "Well-maintained homes"
- "Resident support"
- "Owner confidence"
- "Organized property oversight"

## 2. Integrity of Claims
This is an independent proof-of-work project. The credibility of the pitch relies on the absolute integrity of its claims.
- **No fake testimonials:** Do not invent quotes from owners or residents.
- **No invented property claims:** Do not fabricate properties, locations, or amenities.
- **No unverified performance numbers:** Stick to the publicly verifiable 11,000+ units, 80 properties, and 20+ years of experience.

## 3. Brand & Affiliation Boundaries
- **No official-affiliation implication:** The project must not look like an official Precision Management communication to the public. 
- **Required disclaimer placement:** Every presentation page and export must include the disclaimer clearly stating this is an independent conceptual exploration by Ger’Quia Abner.

## 4. Technical Compliance
- **Noindex/nofollow requirement:** All web pages created for this presentation must include `<meta name="robots" content="noindex,nofollow" />` to ensure search engines do not index the conceptual brand work and confuse it with the official company presence.
